\section{Particle Detectors}
All particle detectors leverage some aspect of particle-matter interactions to identify and measure the properties of particles.
\subsection{Calorimeters}
Measure energy of particles. Made of material where energy will be deposited, ideally converted to photons which can be measured with photomultipliers. Liquid Argon, silicon, lead glass.
\subsection{Drift tubes}
Geiger counters
Can be used to count charged particles. If using timing cicuits, can be used to measure the position of charged particles passing through it. Arrays of drift tubes can be used to reconstruct tracks.
